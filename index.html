Welcome To ECEN

The Elgon Centre for Empowerment Network (ECEN) is an influential non-governmental organization dedicated to empowering individuals and communities, particularly those that are marginalized or underserved. Through education, advocacy, and capacity-building programs, ECEN seeks to foster social change, equality, and sustainable development. The organization's work is deeply rooted in the values of gender sensitivity, equality and justice, partnership and collaboration, respect for women’s rights, integrity, and professionalism. These values guide ECEN in its efforts to support communities, promote human rights, and address key issues like child protection, gender-based violence (GBV) prevention, governance, and social justice.

Our Story

The Elgon Centre for Empowerment Network is a dynamic organization driven by its core values of gender sensitivity, equality, justice, and integrity. Through its comprehensive programs in child protection, GBV prevention and response, governance, and social justice, ECEN plays a pivotal role in addressing the pressing issues faced by marginalized communities. By championing women's rights, promoting good governance, and safeguarding human rights defenders, ECEN continues to lead the charge for social change. The organization's deep commitment to partnership and collaboration ensures that its efforts resonate at both the grassroots and policy levels, making it a key player in the movement toward a more just and equitable society.

About Us

The Elgon Centre for Empowerment Network (ECEN) is an influential non-governmental organization dedicated to empowering individuals and communities, particularly those that are marginalized or underserved. Through education, advocacy, and capacity-building programs, ECEN seeks to foster social change, equality, and sustainable development. The organization's work is deeply rooted in the values of gender sensitivity, equality and justice, partnership and collaboration, respect for women’s rights, integrity, and professionalism. These values guide ECEN in its efforts to support communities, promote human rights, and address key issues like child protection, gender-based violence (GBV) prevention, governance, and social justice.

    • Gender Sensitivity

      ECEN upholds the principle of gender sensitivity in all of its programs. It recognizes that gender inequality often hinders progress, and that the empowerment of women and girls is crucial to achieving broader social development goals. The organization ensures that its initiatives address the specific needs of women, girls, and other gender minorities, fostering an environment where everyone, regardless of their gender, has equal opportunities to thrive.

      • Integrity

        Integrity is a cornerstone of ECEN’s operations. The organization maintains transparency, honesty, and accountability in all of its actions. It adheres to high ethical standards in its dealings with stakeholders, funders, and the communities it serves. ECEN’s commitment to integrity builds trust with its partners and ensures that resources are used effectively to achieve the desired outcomes.

      • Respect for Women’s Right

        ECEN is a staunch advocate for women’s rights and believes that achieving gender equality is central to the well-being of societies. The organization works to promote the protection and advancement of women's rights in all spheres of life, including education, health, political participation, and economic opportunities. ECEN’s programs address issues like gender-based violence, unequal access to resources, and discriminatory practices, ensuring that women and girls can exercise their rights freely and safely.

      • Equality and Justice

        At the heart of ECEN’s mission is the promotion of equality and social justice. ECEN is committed to addressing the systemic inequities that exist within societies, including gender inequality, discrimination based on socio-economic status, and lack of access to resources. Through its work, ECEN strives to level the playing field, ensuring that all individuals, particularly vulnerable groups, have access to their fundamental rights and opportunities for success.

      • Partnership and Collaboration

        ECEN recognizes that sustainable change is best achieved through collective action. The organization collaborates with local communities, government agencies, international organizations, and other stakeholders to create synergies and maximize impact. This collaborative approach allows ECEN to leverage resources, expertise, and networks to reach wider populations and achieve shared goals of empowerment and social change

      • Professionalism

        ECEN approaches its work with the utmost professionalism. This includes maintaining high standards of competence, accountability, and respect for those it serves. The organization continually invests in the professional development of its staff and volunteers, ensuring they are equipped with the knowledge, skills, and tools necessary to carry out their work effectively and ethically.

Child Protection

ECEN is dedicated to the protection of children from all forms of abuse, exploitation, and neglect. Through awareness campaigns, advocacy, and direct interventions, the organization works to ensure that children grow up in safe, supportive environments. It collaborates with schools, families, and communities to strengthen child protection systems and safeguard the rights of children.

GBV Prevention and Response

Gender-based violence is a significant focus area for ECEN. The organization works on both prevention and response efforts by educating communities about the harmful effects of GBV and providing support services for survivors. ECEN implements training programs, develops resource materials, and advocates for stronger policies and laws to prevent GBV and protect survivors. It also provides counseling, legal aid, and shelters for those affected

Governance and Social Justice

ECEN actively engages in promoting good governance and social justice, advocating for policies that ensure transparency, accountability, and equality. The organization works with local governments and civil society to strengthen democratic processes, including promoting fair elections, citizens’ participation, and the rule of law. ECEN's initiatives aim to empower communities to hold leaders accountable and to address issues such as corruption and inequality.

Human Rights Defenders (HRDs) Safeguards

ECEN places a strong emphasis on protecting human rights defenders who work on the front lines of social justice, often at great personal risk. The organization provides support for HRDs, ensuring they have access to legal protection, psychosocial support, and safe spaces. By advocating for the rights and safety of HRDs, ECEN contributes to a global movement that recognizes the importance of these individuals in advancing human rights and social

Advocacy and Awarenes

Raising awareness on key issues affecting vulnerable groups in policy changes and implementation to support sustainable development and Human Rights.

Support for Survivors

ECEN provides critical services for Survivors of violence including counseling, Legal aid and referrals to health care services.

What is gender-based violence?

GBV is any harmful act directed at a person based on their gender, including physical, sexual, or psychological harm, threats, coercion, and deprivation of liberty, primarily affecting women and girls due to power imbalances and gender inequality. .

What are some examples of GBV?

Domestic violence (physical, emotional, sexual abuse within a partnership) Sexual assault Rape Forced marriage Female genital mutilation Sexual harassment Trafficking of women and girls Honor killings

Why is GBV considered a human rights violation?

Because it discriminates against individuals based on their gender, denying them fundamental rights to equality, dignity, and safety.

What are the root causes of GBV?

Gender inequality and harmful gender norms Power imbalances between genders Societal attitudes that condone violence against women Lack of access to justice and support systems

Who can be affected by GBV?

Anyone, regardless of age, socioeconomic status, sexual orientation, or gender identity, but women and girls are disproportionately affected.

What are some signs that someone might be experiencing GBV?

Physical injuries Withdrawal from social activities Changes in behavior or mood Low self-esteem Fearfulness or anxiety Difficulty concentrating

What can be done to prevent GBV?

Promote gender equality and challenge harmful gender norms Educate communities about GBV Provide access to legal and support services for survivors Empower women and girls economically and socially Involve men and boys in prevention efforts

What should you do if you suspect someone is experiencing GBV?

Believe the survivor Offer support and listen without judgment Provide information about available resources and support services Encourage them to seek help from professionals or authorities
